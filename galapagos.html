THE GALAPAGOS ISLANDS

The Galapagos Islands originated from lava eruptions that came from the bottom of the ocean and rose to 2,600 feet above sea level. Some of the younger islands still have active volcanoes. Different characters, such as the isolation, the climate, the altitude and the texture of the land on the islands have made their vegetation different and the animal life unique in the world. However Galapagos is not just about animals and plants. There are a large number of shops, museums, art galleries, bars, clubs, restaurants and hotels that provide even pure fun and entertainment.

WILDLIFE OF GALAPAGOS

The Galapagos Islands have a wide variety of amazing animals and different species. Among the animals found there are the different species of Galapagos giant tortoises, blue-footed and masked boobies, red-footed boobies, flamingos, frigates, albatrosses, unique little penguins, flightless cormorants, and 15 different species of finches. The Galapagos Islands also attract a wide variety of marine mammals, such as sea lions, dolphins, and whales, as well as a wide variety of colorful fish and sharks.The picture shows a baby sea lion, for me, it is the cutest animal of the Galapagos Islands.
